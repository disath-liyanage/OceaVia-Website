Marine plastic pollution is one of the most critical environmental challenges facing the world‚Äôs oceans today. Plastics are durable and degrade slowly, accumulating in oceans and harming marine biodiversity, human health, and ecosystem stability. According to UNEP, marine plastic accounts for 80% of all marine litter, demanding urgent global attention.

üßΩ Sources & Types of Pollution

Major Sources

  • Land-Based: About 80% comes from mismanaged waste, urban runoff, industrial discharges, and littering along rivers and coasts.
  • Sea-Based: About 20% originates from fishing vessels, aquaculture, offshore platforms, and cargo ships.

Types of Plastic Pollution

  • Macroplastics: Large visible items like bags, bottles, fishing nets.
  • Microplastics: Particles smaller than 5mm from synthetic clothing or fragmented plastic.
  • Nanoplastics: Tiny particles under 100nm, increasingly studied for their toxicity.
